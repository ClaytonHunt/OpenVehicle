# Roadster Platform v0.1 Assumptions (Initial)

- **Status:** Draft baseline for concept/CAD work
- **Date:** 2026-04-08

## Purpose

Define practical starting assumptions for the first full-size lightweight roadster platform, without overcommitting to final dimensions before iterative packaging and prototype testing.

## Design Intent

- Two-seat, front-engine or mid-engine compatible architecture (decision deferred)
- Rear-wheel-drive baseline for initial simplicity
- Open-top roadster body with non-structural printed panel system
- Straight-tube chassis using ADR-001 and ADR-002 constraints

## Preliminary Targets (non-binding)

These are working targets, not guarantees:

- **Curb mass target range (prototype):** 700–950 kg, depending on powertrain and safety equipment
- **Wheelbase concept range:** 88–98 in
- **Front/rear track concept range:** 56–64 in
- **Ground clearance target (streetable baseline):** 100–130 mm
- **Occupant package:** 95th percentile male + 5th percentile female accommodation goal (to be validated)

## Chassis Assumptions

- Ladder-perimeter + triangulated bays hybrid architecture (to be refined)
- Defined front and rear module interfaces for future variants
- Removable/serviceable non-structural body panel mounts
- Explicit side-impact and rollover load-path planning, with no safety performance claims until tested

## Fabrication Assumptions

- Flat welding table jig workflow as default
- Common hobbyist tooling baseline:
  - Chop saw/band saw for straight cuts
  - MIG/TIG welding capability
  - Drill press and angle grinder
  - Optional CNC/plasma outsourcing for brackets/gussets
- Tolerance strategy should account for real-world fit-up (not just ideal CAD)

## Suspension/Brakes/Steering (initial constraints)

- Use readily available donor or motorsport-aftermarket components where practical
- Keep pickup hardpoint strategy modular to avoid locking into one donor too early
- Provide datum-based hardpoint tables for traceable revisions

## Powertrain/Electrical (initial constraints)

- Design early packaging envelope to support at least one ICE and one EV pathway in future revisions
- Do not let multi-powertrain support block Phase 1 roadster rolling chassis progress

## Safety and Compliance Notes

- Meeting local registration requirements is a legal process and may include inspections.
- Legal approval does not imply modern OEM crash performance.
- Builders should treat this platform as an engineering project requiring iterative testing and conservative judgement.
